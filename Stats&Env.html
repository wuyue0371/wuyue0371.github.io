Stats&Env

In this page you can find some notes about how stats can help to make the environment better(an interesting topic for me).

🌏 I've always been interested in the idea that one can do data analysis in the environmental field. There are many unresolved issues in the environmental field that require data analysis, a promising land for the application of statistics. During undergraduate, I've also learned some courses related to Geographic Information System (GIS)(hope to learn spatial-temporal analysis and gain techinical skills about Python interaction with ArcGIS).

My first internship was at 北京智慧绿行科技有限公司 where my work focused on the topic of exploring the characteristics of automotive working condition and pollutant emission as well as their relationship based on On-Board Diagnostic(OBD) system data(second-level driving monitoring data of more than 1,000 heavy-duty commercial vehicles in one year).
This issue is crucial for pollution reduction, also being an interesting and challenging problem from a statistical perspective. For example, facing data that is multi-dimensional, densely sampled over time, and of inconsistent quality, how would you preprocss it? How would you draw conclusion combining domain knowledge(in this setting, domain knowledge is related to chemical pollutant emissions) with statistical findings?
Also, during my internship at this company, I realized(or confirmed) that there are many unresolved issues in the environmental field that require the help of statisticians.

My final project of the Time Series Analysis course is about using the methods of time series to analyze S02 pollution.
